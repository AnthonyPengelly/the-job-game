D The mini-game lifecycle

Every obstacle uses one shared three-state lifecycle, designed once and applied to all ten. The stage always splits into STATUS (mode · timer · progress) · CHALLENGE (the hero) · REFEREE (boosts · outcome) so the GM always knows where to look.

1 · Armed / brief

Nothing runs yet

Game name, the GM's read-aloud script, a GM-only difficulty readout, the cards laid out static, and which committed player holds which boost. The timer is shown but NOT running. One big START in the action bar.

2 · Active

The live challenge

Mode shown as colour + iconography (not prose), progress as a meter (not a buried count), boost buttons that surface only for committed holders and fire once then disable — with no layout shift when a boost or state fires (the space is reserved).

3 · Resolve

Outcome control

Clean / Complication / Botched, with the suggested tier pre-selected and the comedic middle one tap away. The GM confirms — the app never overrides — then the Spoils / Wrap-up beat.

E Overlay strategy

Pop controls out only as needed; the stage stays behind. Three patterns, each with a clear job.

Drawer

Slides from an edge, stage stays visible. The full Soundboard and the GM Overrides panel — things the GM dips into mid-performance. Soft scrim.

Popover

Anchored to its trigger. Crew detail / override (from a rail avatar), gear-assign target, commit-crew, dial info.

Dialog

Centred, blocking, scrim. Settings, confirm-destructive (new job / abandon run), reopened gear assignment. The only place the app slows the GM down.

F Two interaction rules

The things the old design got wrong, designed here explicitly.

Arm, then START

Mini-games never run on load. They open ARMED — setup static, timer stopped — and only START makes anything live. Timed rooms must never start the clock until START. (Screens 05a–c, and every game in 05·1–10.)

Spoils is the only gear moment

No permanent "add gear to whom?" tray. After a room, a short closing stage: outcome + sting, Loot and dropped Gear face-up, then pick the holder from a dropdown on each card. Power-ups snap to lane; lane-of-choice cards let the GM pick the lane too. Left unassigned, the Gear launcher badges and reopens as a dialog. (Screen 06.)

No dead-ends

Every tracked value — Heat, Loot, stats, power-ups, exhaustion, outcome, phase — is GM-editable at any time via GM Overrides and the crew popover, with Undo on every change. Any state the app reaches, the GM can edit out of. (Screens 11, 12.)

Transparent rolls

Scenario rolls show the maths before the roll: lane · attempter's rating · base difficulty · resulting DC and odds — in app-roll or physical-die-entry mode. Nothing hidden from the GM. (Screen 04c–d.)
